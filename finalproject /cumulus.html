CUMULUS CLOUDS

1. Cumulus clouds are low-level clouds, generally found less than 2,000 meters above the ground.

2. Cumulus clouds are formed near the earth when hot wet air rises from the ground.

3. Cumulus clouds have a flat base. This is because the accumulated water vapor in the clouds only condenses after it reaches a certain height.

4. There are several types of Cumulus clouds: Cumulus fractus, Cumulus humilis, Cumulus mediocris, Cumulus congestus, and Strato cumulus clouds.

5. The word “Cumulus” means pile in Latin. Cumulus clouds get their name because they look “piled up” over one another.
