CIRRUS CLOUDS

1. Cirrus clouds are generally found at heights above 5,000 meters. These are considered high-level clouds.

2. Cirrus is composed entirely of ice crystals. At this height any air mass that cools to saturation will produce ice crystals rather than water droplets.

3. An extensive area of cirrus, covering a large portion of the sky, may indicate an approaching weather front and a change in the weather.

4. A thunderstorm can pump moisture high up into the troposphere where it freezes into ice crystals.

5. The word "Cirrus" means curl, tuft or wisp of hair in Latin. This is given for its wisp-like formations.
