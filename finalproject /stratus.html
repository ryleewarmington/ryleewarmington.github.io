STRATUS CLOUDS

1. Stratus clouds are generally found less than 2,000 meters above the ground. These are considered low-level clouds.

2. Stratus clouds do not normally produce rain. However, they can still produce mist.

3. Stratus clouds resemble fog, and occasionally when fog lifts, it will form a stratus cloud deck.

4. Stratus clouds form 50% of the annual cloud cover in Arctic regions that affects the energy emissions and absorptions through radiation.

5. The word "Stratus" means layer or to stretch and extend in Latin. These clouds are usually in thick blankets.
